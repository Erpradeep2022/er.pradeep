About Me

I am a doctoral researcher at the National Institute of Technology (NIT), Rourkela, working under the prestigious Chips-to-Startup (C2S) initiative by MeitY.

My research focuses on the design of secure architectures for Industrial IoT. I specialize in ASIC/FPGA implementations of cryptographic algorithms like ASCON and the development of Trusted Platform Modules (TPM) for edge security.

Education:
  • Ph.D. (Electronics & Comm.) - NIT Rourkela (Ongoing)
  • B.Tech (ETC) - PMEC Berhampur (CGPA: 8.61)

Research Interests

Hardware Security

Side-channel analysis, secure IC design, and physically unclonable functions (PUF).

VLSI Design

RTL-to-GDSII flow, low-power optimization, and SCL 180nm technology node implementation.

IIoT Security

Developing hardware-based roots of trust for industrial gateways and edge computing.
